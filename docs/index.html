NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0

The NIST CSF is often misunderstood as a technical checklist. In reality, it is a governance framework designed to facilitate communication between technical and non-technical stakeholders. It organises cybersecurity activities into six functions, and the key course insight is that all five operational functions have relationships with the sixth function, Govern, which integrates them into enterprise decision-making.
Source: Reader Ch. 3.5, College 3, College 4, Reader Ch. 6.5.3

Exam tip: When mapping a case to the NIST CSF, do not just name the function. Explain what specifically failed under that function and what control would fix it. Tutors penalise "control name-dropping without framework placement." Also remember that Govern was added in CSF 2.0 — the older version had only five functions.
⚙

Govern (GV)

Organisational context, risk strategy, roles and responsibilities, and oversight mechanisms that integrate cyber risk into enterprise governance.

▼

Key activities from course materials

ActivityWhat this means
Risk appetiteDefine and communicate risk appetite so all levels of the organisation understand what is acceptable
Governance structureThe Three Lines Model assigns clear accountability — 1st line: operations, 2nd line: risk/compliance, 3rd line: internal audit
Policy hierarchyPolicy → Standard → Procedure → Guideline. Treat these as distinct, not interchangeable (exam anti-pattern #10)
Board engagementUnder NIS2 and DORA, board-level engagement with cybersecurity is a legal requirement, not just good practice
Accountability frameworksSecurity decisions must have named owners, not just committees — otherwise nobody is responsible when things fail
AI governanceThe EU AI Act requires risk classification and oversight throughout the AI system lifecycle

Why Govern is central

Govern is the function that connects all the others to business decision-making. Without Govern, an organisation might have excellent technical controls that nobody oversees, nobody updates, and nobody is accountable for — the exact pattern behind the "Certified but Compromised" case (C03) and the Equifax breach (C07), where known vulnerabilities went unpatched because of governance failures, not technical ones.

Case connection: In the NotPetya/Maersk exam question (Mock Q1), the IT manager argues the board doesn't need to be involved. The model answer says this attitude itself reveals a governance gap — no bridge between technical operations and executive leadership. That is a Govern failure.

Identify (ID)

Understanding and managing organisational assets, data, and processes that need protection. You cannot protect what you do not know you have.

▼

Key activities from course materials

ActivityWhat this means
Asset classification & inventoryWhich systems, data, and processes are critical? You cannot protect what you do not know you have.
Data classificationWhat sensitivity level does each data type have? Especially relevant for GDPR special categories like health data.
Risk assessmentWhat threats apply, what is the likelihood and impact? NIST SP 800-30 provides the methodology.
Business impact analysisHow does loss of each asset affect the business in financial, operational, regulatory, and reputational terms?
Vendor risk assessmentUnderstand dependencies on third parties and their security posture before and during the relationship.
HR processesKnowing who has access to what, especially during role changes and exits — access must follow the person's actual role.

Resilience mapping (Reader Table 6.1)

Identify supports the resilience objective of anticipating and preparing for disruption through asset understanding, risk assessment, business environment analysis, and preparedness planning aligned with mission objectives.

Case connection: In the UvaExpress logistics case (Mock Q3), attackers exploited a single set of credentials to modify shipment data, steal customer info, and disrupt batch processing. The Identify failure was that no asset inventory classified the order-tracking platform as high-value, and no risk assessment had modelled credential theft scenarios.

Protect (PR)

Implementing safeguards to ensure delivery of critical services. This is where preventive controls live — access management, encryption, segmentation, training.

Cross-function dependencies

Protect controls depend on Identify — you need to know what assets you have before you can protect them. College 3 uses access control as the teaching example: it is a "Protect" control that depends on "Identify" controls (asset management, HR processes), feeds input to "Detect" controls (logging of access detects unusual behaviour), and can enforce "Respond" controls (closing down access during an incident).

CIA impact: Encryption increases confidentiality but may slightly reduce availability. MFA improves confidentiality but slightly reduces availability if it fails. Access controls improve confidentiality but may decrease availability if too restrictive. These trade-offs are exam material.

Detect (DE)

Identifying and alerting on cybersecurity events in a timely manner. The bridge between prevention and response — determines whether a breach is found in hours or months.

▼

Key controls mapped in course

ControlWhat it doesKey detail
IDS/IPSIntrusion Detection/Prevention Systems monitor network traffic for malicious patternsPrevention (IPS) blocks; detection (IDS) alerts
SIEMSecurity Information and Event Management aggregates and correlates log data across systemsCentral visibility across the whole environment
Anomaly detectionIdentifies deviations from normal behaviourCritical for insider threats and advanced persistent threats
Log monitoringLogging of access is input for Detect controls to spot unusual behaviourExplicitly taught in College 3 as the link from Protect to Detect
Vulnerability scanningProactively identifies weaknesses before attackers exploit themEquifax had a scanner — but nobody acted on the results
Database activity monitoringTracks who accessed what data and whenEssential for detecting data exfiltration
Access reviewsPeriodic review of who has access to whatBoth a detective and a governance activity
Fraud detectionA compensating control for failing integrity or access controlsCollege 3: compensating controls cover gaps when primary controls fail

Why Detect matters most

In the Mock Exam 2026 Q3 model answer, Detect is called the most beneficial NIST category to prioritise. When preventive controls fail (and they eventually will), Detect determines whether you discover the breach in hours or months. Equifax was exploited for months before detection. Effective detection also improves all other functions through feedback loops — you learn what Protect controls are failing and what Respond playbooks you need.

Compensating controls: Different control types can work together, or one can compensate another when it fails. A firewall is a preventive control, but it also helps detection because it de-noises the network. Fraud detection compensates for failing integrity controls. This interplay is specifically taught in College 3.

Respond (RS)

Taking action on detected incidents to contain damage and coordinate the response across technical, business, legal, and communication channels.

▼

Key activities from course materials

ActivityWhat this meansKey detail
Incident response coordinationWho does what, when, and with what authority?Pre-planned playbooks per scenario type — do not improvise during a crisis
Crisis communicationInternal (staff, board) and external (regulators, customers, media)Maersk CEO's transparency actually improved long-term reputation
Threat containmentIsolating affected systems, revoking access, blocking lateral movementAccess control serves as an enforcement point here (College 3)
Evidence preservationMaintaining forensic integrity for investigation and legal proceedingsPremature system shutdowns can destroy evidence (NordFreight, Exam A Q1)
Regulatory notificationGDPR: breach notification within 72 hoursNIS2 and DORA have their own reporting obligations and timelines
Third-party coordinationInvolving vendors, law enforcement, and external forensicsVendor incident notification SLAs should be contracted in advance

Access control as a Respond enforcement point

College 3 teaches that access control can serve as an enforcement point for Respond — for example, closing down or restricting access to reduce the impact of an ongoing incident. This is a concrete example of how a single control operates across multiple CSF functions simultaneously.

Case connection: In the NordFreight supply chain case (Exam A, Q1), the board wanted to immediately terminate the VesselCore vendor contract. The model answer explains that this is itself a Respond failure — proper containment comes first (isolate, forensics, revoke access), then a structured exit. Cutting off a critical vendor in a panic creates a self-inflicted availability crisis.

Recover (RC)

Restoring capabilities and services that were impaired, and using the incident as a learning opportunity to strengthen future resilience.

▼

Key activities from course materials

ActivityWhat this meansKey detail
Recovery planningDefined RTO (Recovery Time Objective) and RPO (Recovery Point Objective) per critical systemThese must be set before an incident — not decided during one
Backup & restorationTested offline/immutable backups that actually work under pressureHaving backups is not enough — you must prove they restore correctly
Business continuityMaintaining essential functions during recovery through manual procedures, redundancy, or alternative systemsMaersk ran global shipping on paper and WhatsApp for days
Lessons learnedStructured post-incident review to identify root causes and improve controlsWithout this, you repeat the same failure (exam anti-pattern #9)
Continuous improvementFeeding recovery experience back into Govern, Identify, and ProtectThis is the Recover → Govern feedback loop the course emphasises

Resilience mapping (Reader Table 6.1)

Recover serves two resilience objectives: "maintain and restore essential functions" (together with Respond — incident response coordination, recovery planning, backup and restoration, communication to sustain critical operations) and "adapt and improve over time" (together with Govern — lessons learned, control adjustments, strategy refinement, and continuous improvement). This dual role is important because it means Recover is not just about getting systems back online — it is also about getting better.

Case connection: Exam anti-pattern #9 is "one-off reaction with no structural improvement." If your answer describes how an organisation recovers from an incident but does not mention lessons learned or governance improvements, you are missing the Recover-to-Govern feedback loop that the course explicitly emphasises.

Resilience Objectives → CSF 2.0 Functions (Reader Table 6.1)

The course explicitly teaches that resilience is not confined to response and recovery. It emerges from coordinated governance, design, operation, and adaptation across the full cybersecurity lifecycle.

Resilience Objective CSF 2.0 Functions How it works
Establish risk-informed leadership and accountability Govern Organisational context, risk strategy, roles and responsibilities, oversight mechanisms that integrate cyber risk into enterprise governance
Anticipate and prepare for disruption Identify Govern Asset understanding, risk assessment, business environment analysis, preparedness planning aligned with mission objectives
Limit impact and contain incidents Protect Detect Segmentation, access control, continuous monitoring, and anomaly detection to reduce blast radius and enable rapid response
Maintain and restore essential functions Respond Recover Incident response coordination, recovery planning, backup and restoration, communication to sustain critical operations
Adapt and improve over time Recover Govern Lessons learned, control adjustments, strategy refinement, continuous improvement of risk management practices

How Controls Cross Functions — Access Control Example (College 3)

Controls are not isolated within a single CSF function. The course uses access control to demonstrate how a single control type participates in multiple functions simultaneously. Understanding these relationships is essential for exam answers.
CSF Function Access Control's Role What this means in practice
Identify Depends on Identify You need asset management and HR processes to know what to protect and who needs access. Without Identify, access control has no foundation.
Protect Is a Protect control Its primary classification — access control prevents unauthorised access to systems and data.
Detect Feeds input to Detect Logging of access attempts provides the raw data that detection controls use to spot unusual behaviour and anomalies.
Respond Enforcement point for Respond During an incident, access can be revoked or restricted to contain the attack — closing down access reduces impact.

CIA Impact by Control (College 3)

Every control affects the three dimensions of the CIA triad differently. Some controls involve trade-offs — for example, encryption increases confidentiality but may slightly reduce availability. These trade-offs regularly appear in exam questions.

How CSF Relates to Other Course Frameworks

Framework Relationship to CSF When to cite it
ISO/IEC 27001 Compliance certification framework with controls that map to CSF functions. Certification proves process exists, not that security is effective. When the question involves compliance or certification — but never say "we are ISO certified" as a standalone answer (anti-pattern #1)
Three Lines Model Organisational structure for implementing the Govern function — who is accountable at each level When the question asks about roles, accountability, or governance structure
GDPR Data protection regulation focused on personal data. Different purpose from CSF but must be considered alongside it. When personal data is involved — special categories (health data) trigger stricter obligations
NIS2 EU directive for critical infrastructure that aligns with CSF concepts — requires appropriate measures, incident reporting, board oversight When the organisation is essential/important infrastructure or provides critical services
DORA Digital Operational Resilience Act for financial sector — board must own ICT risk, structured vendor oversight required When the organisation is a financial institution or provides ICT services to one
EU AI Act Risk-based AI regulation. AI governance falls under Govern; AI-specific controls span Protect, Detect, and Respond. When AI/ML systems are part of the scenario — classify risk level first (high-risk triggers strictest obligations)
NIST SP 800-53 The detailed control catalog underlying the CSF — provides specific technical and organisational controls per function When you need to be specific about which controls exist within a CSF function
